# Learning Resources for AI/ML

A comprehensive collection of books, courses, websites, and other educational materials to support your AI/ML learning journey.

## Books

### Foundational Books
- **"Python for Data Analysis" by Wes McKinney** - Essential for data manipulation with pandas
- **"Hands-On Machine Learning" by Aurélien Géron** - Practical approach to ML with scikit-learn and TensorFlow
- **"The Elements of Statistical Learning" by Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani, and Jerome Friedman** - Comprehensive statistical learning textbook
- **"Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Christopher Bishop** - Bayesian viewpoint on pattern recognition
- **"Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville** - Comprehensive deep learning textbook

### Mathematics
- **"Mathematics for Machine Learning" by Marc Peter Deisenroth, A. Aldo Faisal, and Cheng Soon Ong** - Mathematical foundations specifically for ML
- **"Introduction to Linear Algebra" by Gilbert Strang** - Essential linear algebra concepts
- **"Think Stats: Probability and Statistics for Programmers" by Allen Downey** - Statistics from a computational perspective

### Specialized Topics
- **"Natural Language Processing with Python" by Steven Bird, Ewan Klein, and Edward Loper** - Using NLTK library
- **"Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications" by Richard Szeliski** - Comprehensive computer vision resource
- **"Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction" by Richard S. Sutton and Andrew G. Barto** - Definitive RL textbook

### Reading Strategy
- Start with survey papers in your area of interest
- Read abstracts to determine relevance
- Focus on methodology sections for implementation details
- Replicate experiments to solidify understanding
